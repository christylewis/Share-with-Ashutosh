Introduction¶

In this report, we will be examining The Movie Database (TMDb) which contains data of over 10,000 movies. The data contains information on how well the movie was received (popularity), as well as the budget, revenue, genre, and many other details about each movie.

Some of the questions we will answer with this anaylsis are below.

  • Is higher popularity associated with higher budgets?
  • What genre of movie has the highest revenue?
  • Are movies that are released in a specific month more popular than others?
  • How has the production of different genres changed over the years?

Conclusions¶

Is higher popularity associated with higher budgets?¶

Based on the chart "Revenue and Budget of Movie Based on Popularity Rating", we can conclude that movies with a "medium" or "high" popularity rating are associated with much larger budgets and revenus. The difference between the budget and the revenue is also much greater for "medium" and "high" movies, making them a much better business investment.

What genre of movie has the highest revenue?¶

The top three genres of movies that produce the highest revenue are: Adventure, Science Fiction, and Fantasy.

Are movies that are released in a specific month more popular than others¶

There is a slight difference in popularity of movies based on release month. Movies that are released during the summer months August, September and October, as well as winter (Jan-April) are slightly less popular than movies that are released during other times of the year. The month with the most popular releases is June.

How have the production of different genres of movies changed over time?¶

The movie genres that have increased the most since 1968 are Comedy, Action, and Drama. Not far behind those three are Thriller and Horror. Thriller, Horror, and Documentary have the sharpest increase (biggest change in shortest amount of time), and stayed relatively low until the mid 2000s, where their production numbers greatly increased.

Drama, Comedy, and Action had a much steadier growth over the 50 year period.
